# Session 10 — Phase 3 Final Review Pass

Audit of the rebuilt assembly after Phase 2's gaps + gear-support build.
Generated by walking the saved Grinder_Assembly.FCStd headless and
inventorying every object's placement + bounding box.

## A. Bugs found

### CRITICAL — rotation direction misconception

The `App.Rotation(Vector(0, 1, 0), -90)` form was used in multiple places
under the (wrong) assumption that **-90° about world +Y axis maps local
+Z to world +X**. Empirically (per the audit bounding boxes), the
correct rotation is **+90°**, not **-90°**. The existing
`add_link_axis_x` helper (which uses +90°) confirmed by example —
CrankBB_ShaftLink positions correctly at X=-70..+70, exactly as
expected.

Wrongly-oriented objects (apex / shaft extending opposite of intended):

| Object | Helper / direct | Intended span | Actual span (from audit) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `Jackshaft_Link` | `add_cyl_x` (-90°) | X=70..310 | X=-170..70 ❌ |
| `BBShell_Link` | inline (-90°) | X=-34..+34 | X=-102..-34 ❌ |
| `PillowBlock_Jackshaft_L` | `add_pillow_block_x` (-90°) | shaft bore along +X from X=70 | bore along -X from X=70 ❌ |
| `PillowBlock_Jackshaft_R` | same | bore along +X from X=272 | bore along -X from X=272 ❌ |
| `BevelGear_Input` | inline (-90°) | apex at X=300 | apex at X=252 ❌ |

Fix: change all four `-90` instances to `+90`, plus restore the
`add_cyl_x` / `add_pillow_block_x` helpers to use the correct sign.

### MEDIUM — right pedal basis vectors form a left-handed frame

In `make_rotation_from_basis`, the right pedal was passed:

```python
x_dir = Vector(0, 1, 0)  # +X → +Y (forward)
y_dir = Vector(1, 0, 0)  # +Y → +X (outboard right)
z_dir = Vector(0, 0, 1)  # +Z → +Z
```

But `cross(x_dir, y_dir) = (0,0,-1)`, not `(0,0,1)`. So this basis has
determinant -1 (a reflection, not a rotation). FreeCAD silently
substitutes some valid rotation that doesn't quite match. The audit
shows right-pedal platform extending in WORLD -Y direction (toward
rider) instead of +Y (toward desk).

Fix: `z_dir = Vector(0, 0, -1)` for the right pedal.

### MEDIUM — crank-arm BB-end bore mounted too inboard

Crank arm BB-end bores are at world X=±44 (10mm outboard of the BB
shell faces at ±34). The actual BB axle ends are at X=±70. Real
cranks attach AT the axle ends, not at the shell faces. The crank
arms visually float in mid-air with a 26mm gap to where they should
be physically mounted.

Fix: set `left_crank_x = -70`, `right_crank_x = +70`.

### LOW — pedal spindle 30mm offset is too far outboard

`PEDAL_SPINDLE_REACH = 30` puts the pedal spindle center 30mm
outboard of the crank-arm pedal-bore. The pedal's spindle is only
25mm long on each side of the boss, so it doesn't even reach the
crank-arm bore. Visually the pedals float disconnected from the
cranks.

Fix: `PEDAL_SPINDLE_REACH = 0` so the spindle center coincides with
the crank-arm pedal-bore (the spindle then passes through the crank
arm, with the platform sitting outboard).

## C. Things that are technically correct but should be reviewed later

- `crank_bb_shaft` (140mm donor) still wraps the BB axle but its bore
  is 0 (solid rod, no axle-internal-bearing detail). Adequate for now.
- `idler_shaft` (80mm donor) — single pillow block on top, no bottom
  bearing. Fine for a low-load idler.
- Pillow blocks don't have actual bearings inserted (just the
  bearing-seat counterbore). Real bearings (6202-2RS, 608-2RS) would
  press in. Visually a hole; functionally complete-enough for layout.

  shaft and extends in +Y, but doesn't physically REACH the back rail
  (gap of ~75mm). The bracket conveys structural intent but won't
  literally bolt to the rail at this geometry. Refine later.
