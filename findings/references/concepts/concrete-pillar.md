## Concrete-column / concrete-furniture aquarium hybrids

> True "concrete column with aquarium AND books on top" pieces don't really exist commercially or in hobbyist circles. The closest references below are either concrete stands sold separately from tanks, or "tank set into bookshelf" (no concrete). **Genuine whitespace.**

- [imitation-example] **SR Aquaristik — Architectural Cement Aquarium Stands** — Commercial GFRC (glass-fiber reinforced concrete) stands and pedestals (30cm, 45cm, 100cm) sold as load-bearing concrete furniture meant to support a separately-purchased aquarium. Closest commercially available example of "concrete column + tank" thinking, though sold as base only. URL: https://sraquaristik.com/products/sr-aquaristik-architectural-cement-aquarium-stands
- [imitation-example] **CaVenza (Florence, Italy)** — Artisanal Italian shop building modular steel/aluminum frames clad in stone slabs, marketed as "monolithic" aquarium furniture. Treats the tank as architectural furniture rather than a glass box on a stand. URL: https://cavenza.com/
- [imitation-example] **PlantedTank.net — "Bookshelf-of-Aquariums" build** — Hobbyist build sequencing four 6-gal Mr. Aqua tanks into a bookshelf with the bottom unit serving as a sump. Not concrete, but the closest documented "tank replaces a shelf section" furniture pattern. URL: https://www.plantedtank.net/threads/bookshelf-of-aquariums.975649/
