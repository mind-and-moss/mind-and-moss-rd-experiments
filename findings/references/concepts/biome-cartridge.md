# References — Biome Cartridge (imitation examples)

Real built work and architectural / commercial precedents for the multi-environment sealed-cartridge concept. Cross-references `concepts/biome-cartridge.md` for the AI-excavation content. See `findings/references/README.md` for tag conventions.

## Multi-biome / sealed-compartment vivariums

> Genuine matches for *fully sealed* multi-biome chassis (with independent humidity per cell) are scarce. Almost everyone in the hobby builds *connected* multi-zone tanks where humidity equalizes — exactly the failure mode Biome Cartridge is engineering against. **The hobby gap is real — this is genuine whitespace.**

- [imitation-example] **AntsCanada — "I Connected My Three Giant Vivariums Together"** — Three full-size vivariums (rainforest, swamp, desert-ish) plumbed together as one display network. Useful as the *anti-pattern*: he deliberately lets atmospheres mix. Watch his footage to see exactly the humidity-drift problem sealed cartridges solve. URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0oo0vlmLjN4
- [imitation-example] **Biosphere 2 (Oracle, AZ)** — Full-scale precedent for the cartridge concept: rainforest, desert, ocean, savanna, mangrove all under one chassis with engineered atmospheric separation between biomes. The architecture (airlocks, separate HVAC per biome) is the literal big-brother of the idea. URL: https://www.makelikeanapeman.com/2020/03/23/the-biosphere-2/
- [imitation-example] **Georgia Aquarium — Explorers Cove** — A single exhibit transitioning visitors through three coastal biomes (estuary, salt marsh, coastal ocean) in adjacent tanks within one architectural shell. Commercial proof that "multiple distinct environments in one chassis" reads as a wow moment. URL: https://www.georgiaaquarium.org/resource-center/stories/explorers-cove-georgia-aquariums-new-coastal-biome-exhibit/
