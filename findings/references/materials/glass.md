# References — Glass

External sources for glass selection, fusing, and slumping. Cross-references `topics/materials/` (no `glass.md` yet there) and `topics/fabrication/glass-fabrication.md`.

## Glass manufacturers (fusing-compatible art glass)

These names came up across multiple AI threads as the standard fusing glass options. Find their official sites for COE specs, color catalogs, and technical guides.

- **Bullseye Glass Co.** — COE 90, the most popular fusing glass system in art studios. Full color range, technical resources, kiln-firing schedules, slump mold guides. The default starting point.
- **System 96** — COE 96 system (Spectrum, Uroboros). Compatible across the COE 96 family. Alternative to Bullseye for studios that prefer this COE system.
- **Spectrum Glass** — part of the COE 96 family. Sheet glass and fusing-compatible.
- **Uroboros Glass** — also COE 96 family. Specialty colors and reactions.

> Reminder from `topics/fabrication/glass-fabrication.md`: pick ONE COE system and stay there. Mixing COE systems = guaranteed cracking during cooling.

## Stained glass (NOT fusing-compatible — different product)

- **Cathedral / opalescent stained glass sheets** — most are COE 82-85, designed for soldered/leaded panels, NOT for fusing. If a supplier doesn't list COE, assume it's traditional stained glass and don't fuse with it.

## Kilns

Brands mentioned in source threads as fusing-suitable. Find current model availability + used market on their official channels.

- **Evenheat Kiln Inc.** — Studio Pro STP, GTS series. US-made, common in hobbyist studios.
- **Paragon Industries** — SC-2 small kilns through larger glass kilns.
- **Skutt** — GM1014 mid-size glass kiln. Common in serious hobbyist / small-studio setups.

> Source threads noted: look for "glass kiln" (heats from top), NOT "ceramic kiln" (heats from sides). Used kilns on Facebook Marketplace often half new price.

## Slump mold suppliers

For hemisphere shapes (the Gem's sphere/hemisphere fused element).

- **Slumpy's** — ceramic hemisphere molds, hobbyist range.
- **Creative Paradise** — slump and drape molds, hobbyist-accessible.
- **Bullseye** — sells slump molds direct alongside their glass.

## Tools mentioned for grinding/finishing

- **Diamond Pacific Tool Corp.** — Tech Master series 8" grinders. Used range mentioned in source threads at $800-1200.
