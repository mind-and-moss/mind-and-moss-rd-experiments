# References — Silicone

External sources for silicone selection (aquarium-safe sealants, mold-making silicones, optical-grade options). Cross-references `topics/materials/silicone.md` and `products/the-machine/gem.md`.

## Aquarium-safe sealants (100% silicone, no mildewcide)

Brands mentioned in source threads as safe for tanks and bioactive enclosures. Verify "I" vs "II" suffix on the tube — fungicide-added formulations (e.g. GE Silicone II) are NOT safe.

- **GE Silicone I** — clear/black, 100% silicone, no mildewcide. The most-mentioned aquarium-safe option.
- **Aqueon Silicone Sealant** — sold specifically for aquariums. Easy to confirm safety on label.
- **ASI Aquarium Silicone** (American Sealants Inc.) — industrial / professional aquarium-grade.

> CRITICAL: GE Silicone II contains mildewcide and is NOT aquarium-safe. The "I" vs "II" distinction matters.

## Mold-making silicones (tin-cure vs platinum-cure)

Industry brands hobbyists use for casting molds. Find their datasheets for cure inhibitor lists, working time, Shore hardness.

- **Smooth-On** — Mold Star (platinum), Oomoo (tin), Rebound (skin) lines. The default hobbyist brand for mold-making silicones. Has extensive technical guides and YouTube content.
- **Polytek** — alternative to Smooth-On, similar product range.

## Optical-grade / structural silicones (for invisible-seam panels)

The Machine's gem-component invisible-seam construction (`products/the-machine/gem.md`) needs silicones with optical clarity AND structural strength. The AI threads were vague here — this is a strong "need real datasheets" topic.

- [unvetted] **Dow Corning structural silicones** — used in glass curtain wall construction. Worth investigating for bond-line strength specs.
- [unvetted] **Wacker structural silicones** — same category, European glass-construction supplier.

Source threads listed common platinum-cure silicone inhibitors. When picking a master material for The Gem hardscape molds, cross-check supplier datasheets:

Inhibitors confirmed in source threads: latex, sulfur clays (Plastilina, Roma Plastilina), tin-cure residue, certain spray paints/lacquers, polyester resins, uncured SLA resin, superglue residue.
