# References — Silicone

External sources for silicone selection (aquarium-safe sealants, mold-making silicones, optical-grade options). Cross-references `topics/materials/silicone.md` and `products/the-gem/seam-construction.md`.

## Aquarium-safe sealants (100% silicone, no mildewcide)

Brands mentioned in source threads as safe for tanks and bioactive enclosures. Verify "I" vs "II" suffix on the tube — fungicide-added formulations (e.g. GE Silicone II) are NOT safe.

- **GE Silicone I** — clear/black, 100% silicone, no mildewcide. The most-mentioned aquarium-safe option.
- **Aqueon Silicone Sealant** — sold specifically for aquariums. Easy to confirm safety on label.
- **ASI Aquarium Silicone** (American Sealants Inc.) — industrial / professional aquarium-grade.

> CRITICAL: GE Silicone II contains mildewcide and is NOT aquarium-safe. The "I" vs "II" distinction matters.

## Mold-making silicones (tin-cure vs platinum-cure)

Industry brands hobbyists use for casting molds. Find their datasheets for cure inhibitor lists, working time, Shore hardness.

- **Smooth-On** — Mold Star (platinum), Oomoo (tin), Rebound (skin) lines. The default hobbyist brand for mold-making silicones. URL: https://www.smooth-on.com/
- **Smooth-On Mold Star 30 product page** — Datasheet, mix ratio, hardness for Mold Star 30 platinum silicone. URL: https://www.smooth-on.com/products/mold-star-30/
- **Smooth-On OOMOO 30 product page** — Datasheet for OOMOO 30 tin-cure silicone. URL: https://www.smooth-on.com/products/oomoo-30/
- **Smooth-On OOMOO 25/30 technical bulletin PDF** — Direct technical bulletin covering pot life, cure, working properties for both OOMOO grades. URL: https://www.smooth-on.com/tb/files/OOMOO_25_30_TB.pdf
- **Polytek** — alternative to Smooth-On, similar product range.

## Optical-grade / structural silicones (for invisible-seam panels)

The Gem's invisible-seam construction (`products/the-gem/seam-construction.md`) needs silicones with optical clarity AND structural strength. The AI threads were vague here — this is a strong "need real datasheets" topic.

- **Dow Silicone Structural Glazing Manual (EMEAI) PDF** — Comprehensive Dow manual including bond-line sizing, design wind-load tables, and panel-size load worked examples. **CRITICAL for The Gem's seam-construction sizing — Dow's minimum glueline is 6 mm (1/4"), maximum 13 mm (1/2"), per DOWSIL 983 Guide Specs. Two orders of magnitude tighter than what AI threads suggested.** URL: https://www.dow.com/documents/62/62-0/62-0979-01-structural-glazing-manual.pdf
- **DOWSIL 995 Silicone Structural Sealant TDS PDF** — TDS for the workhorse one-component structural silicone. URL: https://www.buildsite.com/pdf/dowcorning/DOWSIL-995-Silicone-Structural-Sealant-Product-Data-2407402.pdf
- **DOWSIL 895 Structural Glazing Sealant TDS PDF** — TDS for the alternative structural-glazing grade. URL: https://www.buildsite.com/pdf/dowcorning/DOWSIL-895-Structural-Glazing-Sealant-Product-Data-2273066.pdf
- **DOWSIL 983 Guide Specifications PDF** — Two-component structural glazing sealant; the spec doc that defines the 6 mm min / 13 mm max glueline rule. URL: https://www.buildsite.com/pdf/dowcorning/DOWSIL-983-Structural-Glazing-Sealant-Base-and-Curing-Agent-Guide-Specifications-2152197.pdf
- **DOWSIL 795 TDS PDF** — Single-component structural silicone (more common for retrofit / smaller jobs). URL: https://www.dow.com/content/dam/dcc/documents/en-us/productdatasheet/63/63-12/63-1217-dowsil-795-structural-glazing.pdf
- **Wacker Elastosil overview PDF** — Wacker's grade overview covering Elastosil sealant family used in glazing. URL: https://www.wacker.com/h/medias/6009-EN.pdf
- **ASTM C1184 — Structural Silicone Sealants Spec** — The standard that governs structural silicone material specs. URL: https://store.astm.org/c1184-05.html
- **ASTM C1401 — Structural Sealant Glazing Guide** — The standard that governs structural glazing design and bond-line sizing. URL: https://www.astm.org/c1401-07.html

## Cure-inhibitor reference

Source threads listed common platinum-cure silicone inhibitors. When picking a master material for The Gem hardscape molds, cross-check supplier datasheets:

Inhibitors confirmed in source threads: latex, sulfur clays (Plastilina, Roma Plastilina), tin-cure residue, certain spray paints/lacquers, polyester resins, uncured SLA resin, superglue residue.
