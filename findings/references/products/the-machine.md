# References — The Machine (imitation examples)

Real built work, named builders, and commercial / research references that resemble pieces of what The Machine is targeting. Cross-references `products/the-machine/` for the AI-excavation content. See `findings/references/README.md` for tag conventions.

## Multi-tank racetrack / loop aquariums

- [imitation-example] **The King of DIY (Joey Mullen) — Triple aquarium fish bridge** — Build connecting three tanks with two clear acrylic bridges so clown loaches swim a continuous circuit between tanks. Closest hobby-scale version of Isaiah's racetrack idea. URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=seBch2HWlZs
- [imitation-example] **Infinity Aquarium Design — 1200-gallon Race Track Reef** — Commercial racetrack-shaped reef tank (Las Vegas firm) where fish swim a continuous loop around a central island. Aesthetically a faux reef, but the geometry is exactly the racetrack. URL: https://infinityaquarium.com/1200-gallon-race-track-with-faux-reef/
- [imitation-example] **ZIRC (Zebrafish International Resource Center) recirculating rack** — Two-faced rack system with 2×49 3.6 L tanks all plumbed into one shared recirculating water system. Engineering reference for zebrafish-specific multi-tank flow (aesthetics are wrong but the engineering is the most relevant precedent). URL: https://www.researchgate.net/figure/ZIRC-racks-and-tanks-ZIRC-uses-two-faced-rack-systems-with-2x49-36-L-tanks-A-Side_fig2_304522801

## Segmented / circular acrylic tank construction

- [imitation-example] **Brad Newton — "Building an Acrylic Aquarium" (Professional Plastics PDF)** — Long-form documented build covering cell-cast acrylic, edge prep (120 to 400 grit), and Weld-On solvent welding of flat panels. The reference text most DIY tank builders cite. URL: https://www.professionalplastics.com/professionalplastics/BuildingAnAquarium2.pdf
- [imitation-example] **Instructables — "Building an Hexagonal Acrylic Aquarium"** — Flat-panel polygonal tank (hexagon) bonded with acrylic cement; the closest published DIY analog to a segmented circular tank made of flat facets. URL: https://www.instructables.com/Building-an-Hexagonal-Acrylic-Aquarium/

> Gap noted by the research pass: no strong matches for true PVC-connector segmented circular builds. This is genuinely uncharted DIY territory — possible Mind and Moss differentiator.

## Industrial-aesthetic / brutalist aquariums

- [imitation-example] **Tatiana Bilbao Estudio — Sea of Cortez Research Center, Mazatlán** — Monumental rose-tinted raw-concrete aquarium building with cenote-shaped light wells; "flooded ruin" concept. Reigning brutalist-meets-living-water reference. URL: https://www.archpaper.com/2024/04/tatiana-bilbao-estudio-designs-an-aquarium-in-mazatlan-mexico-around-the-premise-of-a-flooded-ruin/
- [imitation-example] **Todd's 2500-gallon basement cement stingray tank (MonsterFishKeepers, 2005)** — 17 ft × 6.5 ft × 4.5 ft poured-concrete tank, 6"-thick rebar-reinforced walls, exposed sump plumbing, dual acrylic windows. Documented build journal — the literal "concrete machine" hobby reference. URL: https://www.monsterfishkeepers.com/forums/threads/todd-builds-his-2500-gallon-cement-aquarium-for-stingrays-in-the-basement.23529/
- [imitation-example] **New England Aquarium (1969, Cambridge Seven Associates)** — Original brutalist exposed-concrete interior wraps the central Giant Ocean Tank; living tank set inside raw concrete architecture.

## O2 saturator / oxygen reactor builds

- [imitation-example] **Freedom Fish Farms — DIY Speece cone install (Facebook, 2020)** — Working farm fabricated and installed a Speece cone with a venturi pre-injector, posted construction photos and tuning notes. Real built unit by named operation. URL: https://www.facebook.com/FreedomFishFarms/posts/674960033255685
- **Dr. Richard Speece (Vanderbilt) — Speece cone background (Global Seafood Alliance)** — Inventor reference for the down-flow bubble contactor; write-up shows real installations and operating parameters (25-50 mg/L DO, ≤21 psi limits). Engineering source, not pure imitation. URL: https://www.globalseafood.org/advocate/speece-cones-saturate-water-with-oxygen-ozone/
- **Robert Strange (UTK WFS 556) — DIY three-pipe stepped saturator** — Course materials describe the homebrew approximation: three PVC pipes of increasing diameter union-fitted to mimic a cone's velocity gradient. Clear DIY recipe. URL: http://web.utk.edu/~rstrange/wfs556/html-content/18-ox-cont.html

# Gem-component references

The following sections are imitation references for the **gem-component within The Machine** — see `products/the-machine/gem.md` for the AI-excavation content. (These were originally in `references/products/the-gem.md` when The Gem was treated as a standalone product. Folded in here 2026-05-05.)

## Fused / kiln-cast / optical-glass centerpiece

- [imitation-example] **Christopher Ries — Hawk Galleries** — Carves monolithic optical lead-crystal blocks (some 1,000+ lbs) into polished abstract forms that reflect internally and transmit "apparitions of light." Strongest reference for a centerpiece optical-glass form, even if his shapes lean abstract rather than spherical. URL: https://hawkgalleries.com/artist/christopher-ries/
- [imitation-example] **Harry Pollitt — Pollitt Studio (Santa Fe, NM)** — Kiln-cast glass sculptures using lost-wax process; flowing hand-sanded forms in jade green and amber that transmit and color light. Closest serious-artist reference for the kiln-cast optical-glass aesthetic. URL: https://pollittstudio.com/portfolio/available-glass/
- [imitation-example] **Scott Hronich — Internal Fire Glass** — One-of-a-kind borosilicate vortex spheres handcrafted with metal oxides. Real maker producing solid spherical art glass. URL: https://internalfireglass.com/

> Gap: no strong match for someone making *fused/slumped* spheres specifically. Kiln-cast and borosilicate paths above are the closest verifiable references.

## Invisible-seam glass panels (UV-bonded / mitered)

- [imitation-example] **Jack Storms — Chroma Cube / Spectrum Cube** — Cold-glass sculptor laminating lead crystal and dichroic glass through hundreds of cuts and polishes per piece. Verified $8,600 cube product page; the laminated construction is the closest commercial reference for "glass bonded by nothing." URL: https://jackstorms.com/products/chroma-cube
- [imitation-example] **Ultum Nature Systems (UNS) rimless cubes** — 45° mitered, low-iron Diamant glass tanks bonded with high-performance silicone. Not as thin as optical adhesive, but the production-grade aquarium reference for minimal-seam construction. URL: https://shop.glassaqua.com/collections/rimless-aquariums-cube
- [imitation-example] **ADA Cube Garden** — The long-running rimless aquarium standard, ultra-clear low-iron glass with thin silicone bonds. Industry benchmark for "the seam should disappear" in aquarium form. URL: https://aquaforestaquarium.com/products/ada-cube-garden-60p-aquarium-ultra-high-clarity-glass

## Mycelium-bound base / biomaterial composite (applies to Machine's overall base too)

- [imitation-example] **Maria Schechter — "The Gatekeepers" (Arrowmont, 2023 juried exhibition)** — Mycelium sculpture grown with indigo dye and Japanese Maple resin; offered to Arrowmont after the "Degrees of Commitment" exhibition and now decomposing outdoors on the school's grounds in Gatlinburg, TN. Direct parallel to the biomaterial-base concept. URL: https://arrowmont.org/arrowmont-welcomes-bio-artist-maria-schechters-mycelium-sculpture-the-gatekeepers-to-campus/
- [imitation-example] **Maria Schechter — interview at Influx Gallery** — Long-form interview about her mycelium-art practice in general (process, materials, philosophy). Background context. URL: https://www.influx-gallery.com/interviews/maria-schechter
- [imitation-example] **Phil Ross — Mycotectural Alpha (Kunsthalle Düsseldorf, 2009)** — 6×6 ft shelter grown from reishi mycelium bricks, stronger pound-for-pound than concrete. Original reference for structural mycelium sculpture. URL: https://www.moma.org/interactives/exhibitions/2013/designandviolence/mycotecture-phil-ross/
- [imitation-example] **The Living / Ecovative — MoMA PS1 "Hy-Fi" (2014)** — 40-ft tower of 10,000 mycelium bricks grown from agricultural waste, with light-refracting 3M film inserts. The canonical institutional precedent for mycelium-as-structure. URL: https://www.archdaily.com/521266/hy-fi-the-organic-mushroom-brick-tower-opens-at-moma-s-ps1-courtyard

## Combined open terrarium + closed aquarium / sculptural paludarium

- [imitation-example] **SerpaDesign (Tanner) — paludarium playlist** — Working maker with dozens of build-log videos combining aquarium and terrarium in single tanks. URL: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLGPT1t4CqpfiPXahgre_hVWtId4eP7bUm
- [imitation-example] **Custom Aquariums × SerpaDesign — 350-gallon paludarium build** — 4-foot-tall tank with ~2 ft of water below and terrestrial planting above; documented two-part build. URL: https://www.customaquariums.com/videos/scaping-the-350-gallon-paludarium-build-part-1/
- [imitation-example] **DOOA (ADA's vivarium brand)** — Japanese product line specifically for emersed/submerged hybrid displays. Wabi-Kusa and Neo Glass containers. URL: https://www.dooa.jp/en/
