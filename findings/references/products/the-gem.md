# References — The Gem (imitation examples)

Real built work, named artists, and commercial products that resemble pieces of what The Gem is targeting. Cross-references `products/the-gem/` for the AI-excavation content. See `findings/references/README.md` for tag conventions.

## Fused / kiln-cast / optical-glass centerpiece

- [imitation-example] **Christopher Ries — Hawk Galleries** — Carves monolithic optical lead-crystal blocks (some 1,000+ lbs) into polished abstract forms that reflect internally and transmit "apparitions of light." Strongest reference for a centerpiece optical-glass form, even if his shapes lean abstract rather than spherical. URL: https://hawkgalleries.com/artist/christopher-ries/
- [imitation-example] **Harry Pollitt — Pollitt Studio (Santa Fe, NM)** — Kiln-cast glass sculptures using lost-wax process; flowing hand-sanded forms in jade green and amber that transmit and color light. Closest serious-artist reference for the kiln-cast optical-glass aesthetic. URL: https://pollittstudio.com/portfolio/available-glass/
- [imitation-example] **Scott Hronich — Internal Fire Glass** — One-of-a-kind borosilicate vortex spheres handcrafted with metal oxides. Real maker producing solid spherical art glass. URL: https://internalfireglass.com/

> Gap noted by the research pass: no strong match found for someone making *fused/slumped* spheres specifically. Kiln-cast and borosilicate paths above are the closest verifiable references.

## Invisible-seam glass panels (UV-bonded / mitered)

- [imitation-example] **Jack Storms — Chroma Cube / Spectrum Cube** — Cold-glass sculptor laminating lead crystal and dichroic glass through hundreds of cuts and polishes per piece. Verified $8,600 cube product page; the laminated construction is the closest commercial reference for "glass bonded by nothing." URL: https://jackstorms.com/products/chroma-cube
- [imitation-example] **Ultum Nature Systems (UNS) rimless cubes** — 45° mitered, low-iron Diamant glass tanks bonded with high-performance silicone. Not as thin as optical adhesive, but the production-grade aquarium reference for minimal-seam construction. URL: https://shop.glassaqua.com/collections/rimless-aquariums-cube
- [imitation-example] **ADA Cube Garden** — The long-running rimless aquarium standard, ultra-clear low-iron glass with thin silicone bonds. Industry benchmark for "the seam should disappear" in aquarium form. URL: https://aquaforestaquarium.com/products/ada-cube-garden-60p-aquarium-ultra-high-clarity-glass

## Mycelium-bound base / biomaterial composite

- [imitation-example] **Maria Schechter — "The Gatekeepers" (Arrowmont, 2023 juried exhibition)** — Mycelium sculpture grown with indigo dye and Japanese Maple resin; offered to Arrowmont after the "Degrees of Commitment: Climate, Ecosystems, and Society" exhibition and now decomposing outdoors on the school's grounds in Gatlinburg, TN. Direct parallel to The Gem's "biomaterial base under crystalline top." URL: https://arrowmont.org/arrowmont-welcomes-bio-artist-maria-schechters-mycelium-sculpture-the-gatekeepers-to-campus/
- [imitation-example] **Maria Schechter — interview at Influx Gallery** — Long-form interview about her mycelium-art practice in general (process, materials, philosophy). Background context for studying her work. URL: https://www.influx-gallery.com/interviews/maria-schechter
- [imitation-example] **Phil Ross — Mycotectural Alpha (Kunsthalle Düsseldorf, 2009)** — 6×6 ft shelter grown from reishi mycelium bricks, stronger pound-for-pound than concrete. Original reference for structural mycelium sculpture. URL: https://www.moma.org/interactives/exhibitions/2013/designandviolence/mycotecture-phil-ross/
- [imitation-example] **The Living / Ecovative — MoMA PS1 "Hy-Fi" (2014)** — 40-ft tower of 10,000 mycelium bricks grown from agricultural waste, with light-refracting 3M film inserts. The canonical institutional precedent for mycelium-as-structure; bricks were composted after the summer exhibition. URL: https://www.archdaily.com/521266/hy-fi-the-organic-mushroom-brick-tower-opens-at-moma-s-ps1-courtyard

## Combined open terrarium + closed aquarium / sculptural paludarium

- [imitation-example] **SerpaDesign (Tanner) — paludarium playlist** — Working maker with dozens of build-log videos combining aquarium and terrarium in single tanks; tutorial-grade documentation of the technique stack. URL: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLGPT1t4CqpfiPXahgre_hVWtId4eP7bUm
- [imitation-example] **Custom Aquariums × SerpaDesign — 350-gallon paludarium build** — 4-foot-tall tank with ~2 ft of water below and terrestrial planting above; documented two-part build. Closest large-format reference to the vertical "aquarium below / terrarium above" stack. URL: https://www.customaquariums.com/videos/scaping-the-350-gallon-paludarium-build-part-1/
- [imitation-example] **DOOA (ADA's vivarium brand)** — Japanese product line specifically for emersed/submerged hybrid displays. Wabi-Kusa and Neo Glass containers. Most refined commercial precedent for treating a paludarium as a single sculptural object. URL: https://www.dooa.jp/en/
