## The 4-SKU matrix
**Open / Sealed × Small / Large = 4 product lines**

|  | Small | Large |
|---|---|---|
| **Open** (vented) | Open Small | Open Large |
| **Sealed** | Sealed Small | Sealed Large |

This grid keeps the customer-facing decision tree simple: pick a vibe (open vs sealed) and pick a footprint (small vs large).

## The "anchor plant" / "holder plant" concept
One plant ships with every kit so customers can build around a known reliable base.

### Top candidate: Heartleaf Philodendron (*Philodendron hederaceum*)
- Tolerant, forgiving, roots easily
- Looks premium, grows into the customer-built framework
- Open/vented: ✅
- Sealed: ⚠️ (can rot if constantly wet + no airflow)
- Light: low → bright indirect (best: medium)
- Humidity: average home → high (with airflow)
- Water: let top dry slightly, hates soggy substrate

### Alternative: split into 2 anchor SKUs
- "Open/Vented Holder Plant" (Heartleaf Philodendron or similar)
- "Sealed Holder Plant" (something more humidity-tolerant)
- Still feels like one choice to customers because it matches the container style

## Tools/inputs available for production line
- **xtool laser cutter** — cuts thin acrylic. Confirmed as a "huge unlock" for terrarium walls.
- **Frog daddy moss mix** — grows moss on anything
- **68oz square deli containers** — standard small cell (Isaiah has ~30 of them)
- **Acrylic sheets + small table saw + precision ruler** — for custom panel work
