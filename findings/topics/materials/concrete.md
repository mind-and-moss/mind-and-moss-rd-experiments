## Concrete + acrylic hybrid construction
- **The principle:** "Let the concrete carry the load, let the acrylic act as windows."
- This is how large public aquariums think (scaled down).
- Pressure: ~0.43 psi per foot of water height.
- A true unsupported 10-gallon wall CANNOT be safely made from 1mm acrylic for 5-year service.
- Concrete = pressure vessel. Acrylic = viewport.

## Acrylic-to-concrete bonding
- Different game from acrylic-to-acrylic — flex-tolerant gasket joint between two very different materials.
- Acrylic: flexible, smooth, low surface energy.
- Concrete: rigid, porous, slightly moving with moisture.
- **Aquarium silicone wins** as the bonding agent — flexible, tolerates differential movement, excellent water resistance, bonds well to porous concrete, easy to service, long track record.
- Public aquariums often use silicone gaskets even with acrylic windows.

## Fiber concrete in aquariums
- Bare vs sealed (sealants/resins) comparison explored
- Fiber reinforcement helps with crack resistance under tank pressure cycles
- Stainless steel rebar reinforcement viable for larger forms

## Concrete cylinder molds
- Custom curved concrete forms can be made with mold work
- Achievable shapes include staircase forms

## Sandwich stack pattern (for the concrete pillar concept)
Vertical layers:
1. Glass
2. Wood puck
3. Concrete cylinder (load-bearing)
4. Wood puck
5. Glass

Middle layer (wood pucks + concrete cylinder) is resin-coated. Whole column gets encased in resin then sanded smooth.

This pattern feeds the `concepts/concrete-pillar.md` concept.
