# Humidity & Airflow

Cross-cutting research on managing humidity and airflow in sealed and semi-sealed terraria. Production-scale notes (consolidating many small terraria) live here too.

Source: RESEARCH-005 Thread 26 — "Humidity Control for Tanks" (chat #29)

## Tank battery problem (production scale)
**Setup:** ~20 mini terrariums in mini deli containers (some open) plus a 20-gallon aquarium. Water evaporation = smell problem.

### Strategy
- **Close open containers first** (prioritize the room over plant access)
- Larger sealed system reduces exposed water surface area → less evaporation, less smell
- **Wire rack with one larger unified system** beats many tiny containers
- 68oz square deli containers as the standard production cell
- Repurpose smaller containers for other uses

### Implication for production line
- Standardize on 68oz cubes for inventory pieces.
- One bigger consolidated wet-mass per shelf instead of many small ones — controls smell at scale.

## Can airflow + high humidity coexist?
Yes — and it's required for mold/algae control in any sealed terrarium that runs for weeks.

## Mist makers as airflow drivers
- Mist makers can drive airflow in sealed-but-vented terraria
- Reliable and effective when paired with even minimal venting

## Air pump-driven sealed environment
- Can run for weeks-on-end if properly designed
- Pairs naturally with the biome cartridge concept (each cartridge gets its own air pump loop)

## Styrofoam-box vivariums (observed pattern)
- Other builders building 4ft+ vivariums from styrofoam
- Single door cutout with glass sheet placed between cut-out grooves
- Glass sealed with silicone to the foam
- Foam lined with chemical (likely sealant or hardener)
- Stability concerns at 4ft length (covered in `concepts/styrofoam-vivarium.md`)
