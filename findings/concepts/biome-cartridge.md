## What's known so far

### The non-negotiable rule
**Each environment needs its own sealed atmosphere — or it will drift toward the wettest one.**

Without sealing:
- Humidity equalizes (tropical wants 80-100% RH, desert wants 20-40% — shared air = 50% mush in both)
- CO₂ / O₂ equalize over time
- Water vapor migrates from any warm wet zone into everything else

### Two architecture options explored

**Option A: Humidity-spectrum grid (9×2 or 9×3 cells)**
- Each cell holds a different RH (40% → 95% across the spectrum)
- Air isolation per chamber (any gaps → diffusion equalization)
- Interconnects must be liquid-only (no shared headspace) OR use gas flow restrictors + buffers
- **Salt solutions create stable RH setpoints** (classic lab trick — different salts hold different RH at the same temp)
- Or: desiccant packs for low RH cells, open water/wet media for high RH cells
- This is the only way to get a stable "spectrum" without active control

**Option B: 4 distinct biomes in one chassis**
- Underwater + desert + tropical + open field (half shade)
- Single outer chassis, 4 independent "biome cartridges" inside
- Each cartridge: own RH/temp/light/photoperiod control
- **Per-environment design specs:**
  - Tropical: 80-100% RH, stable warmth
  - Desert: 20-40% RH, warm days / cooler nights
  - Underwater: usually buffered but can heat
  - Open field: half-shade, moderate RH

### Supporting tech explored in the same thread
- DIY condenser for humidity control
- Self-contained biosphere principles
- Dual-tank setup with small connector tube to limit humidity transfer
- Fan + copper tubing / metal plate cooling for water temperature
- 5-gallon bucket as integrated gas + filter chamber (cross-references the canister filter concept)

### Dehumidifier coupling (for closed-system humidity escape)
- 15-gallon aquarium with all airflow routed out one tube → external dehumidifier
- Cab Yick $45 dehumidifier on Amazon mentioned for prototyping
