# Concrete Aquarium-Furniture Column — Concept

> **Pitch:** A load-bearing concrete column that integrates a glass aquarium and supports books or other weight on top. Architectural aquarium-furniture hybrid.

## Why it might matter
- Treats the aquarium not as a freestanding tank but as a piece of furniture.
- Concrete column is the structural element; the aquarium is integrated into it; books rest on top.
- Combines industrial brutalism + living ecosystem (matches Mind and Moss design philosophy).
- Multi-purpose footprint — single object replaces a side table or bookshelf section + an aquarium.

Source: RESEARCH-005 Thread 24 — "Fiber Concrete vs Sealing" (chat #2 — 162 turns)

## What's known so far

### Material approach
- Fiber concrete IN aquariums: bare vs sealed (sealants/resins) tradeoffs explored
- Concrete cylinder molds: making custom curved concrete forms
- Concrete aquarium body with stainless steel rebar reinforcement, glass windows for viewing
- Achievable shapes: including a staircase form

### Sandwich stack design (one viable construction pattern)
Vertical layers from top to bottom:
1. Glass
2. Wood puck
3. Concrete cylinder (the load-bearing core)
4. Wood puck
5. Glass

Middle layer (wood pucks + concrete cylinder) is resin-coated. Whole column is then sanded smooth. Glass forms the viewing top and bottom.

### Load-bearing logic
- Constant pressure on the column comes from the box of glass weight + books on top
- All seals: silicone
- The whole column gets encased in resin then sanded smooth for finish

### Mounting strategies considered
- Structure behind the aquarium connects to the aquarium via PVC pipe
- Welding onto thin acrylic discussed
- Glass-touching-glass spacing with silicone fill
- Spray paint considered but Isaiah was reluctant to add new materials

## Open questions
- Books-on-top weight target (how heavy a load is being designed for?)
- Earthquake / vibration tolerance (Fresno, CA isn't seismic-free)
- Water temperature impact on concrete-glass-resin sandwich (thermal expansion mismatch)
- Maintenance access — how do you service the aquarium when it's structurally integrated?
- Is this a one-off centerpiece or a SKU? If SKU, what's the standard size and weight class?
