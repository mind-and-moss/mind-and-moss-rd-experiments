# Styrofoam-Box Vivarium Chassis — Concept

> **Pitch:** Large-format vivariums (4ft+) built from styrofoam boxes lined with hardener/sealant, with glass viewing panels in cut-out grooves. Cheap, lightweight, scalable construction pattern for big terraria.

## Why it might matter
- Glass / acrylic at 4ft+ is heavy, fragile, and expensive.
- Styrofoam + sealant + glass viewing panel keeps weight low and cost low at large scale.
- Existing builders are doing this — it's a proven pattern Mind and Moss could productize / brand.

Source: RESEARCH-005 Thread 27 — "Airflow in High Humidity" (chat #31 — 39 turns)

## What's known so far (from observing another builder)

### Construction pattern
- Styrofoam boxes (insulation foam, like the pink rigid stuff)
- Single door cutout with a glass sheet placed between cut-out grooves
- Glass sealed with silicone to the foam
- Foam lined with a chemical layer — likely a sealant or hardener (epoxy / fiberglass resin family)
- Builds up to ~4 feet long

### Stability concerns at 4ft length
- Long styrofoam isn't structurally rigid by itself
- The hardener layer + glass face provides the rigidity
- Open question: how to keep a 4ft box from bowing inward when filled with substrate + water

### Airflow + mist makers (paired with this build pattern)
- Mist makers can drive airflow in sealed-but-vented terraria
- Air pump-driven sealed environment can run for weeks-on-end

## Open questions
- Specific sealant/hardener for styrofoam → safe for plants/animals?
- 5-year longevity of styrofoam-bonded glass joint
- Maintenance: how do you re-open a glass-in-groove panel without breaking the seal?
- Brand angle: cheap-and-cheerful "starter vivarium" vs premium "boutique large-format" — which fits Mind and Moss better?
