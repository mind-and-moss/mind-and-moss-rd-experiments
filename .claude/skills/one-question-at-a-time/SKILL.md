# One question at a time

Voice mode is single-threaded. Isaiah will not hold a list in his head.

## The exact failure mode this prevents

From the bike-grinder Phase 0 voice session, after I sent 7 numbered questions:

> "Let's do one question at a time. I'm not gonna remember all that shit."

He's not being snippy — he's accurately describing voice mode. Even on text, when he's driving or scattered, multi-question messages get the first question answered (maybe) and the rest lost.

## The rule

When Isaiah is in voice mode or you have any signal he's busy, **ask exactly one question per message.** No list. No "and one more thing." One question, answered, then the next question.

## Signals that voice mode / busy mode is on

- Last message had transcription chop ("I would like to stay relatively relaxed during this. I could stay pretty relaxed in, uh, like, um...")
- He just told you he's driving / at school / out of the house
- His messages are short and fragmentary
- He's said "Hello?" or "What was that?" recently (means he tuned out)
- He explicitly asked "one at a time"

## How to phrase a single question

- Make it answerable in one short sentence
- Avoid compound questions ("Is X right, and if so, do you want Y or Z?" — that's two questions)
- Avoid open-ended exploratory questions ("What do you think about...") — too much cognitive load
- Make it a binary or 3-option pick when possible

**Bad:** "What's the longest single glass edge on any Machine panel, and does that drive the platen length, and do you want to bump from 24" to 28" if needed?"

**Good:** "What's the longest single glass edge on any Machine panel?"

(Wait for answer, then: "Got it. Does that mean we can stay at 24" platen, or do you want to bump?")

## When you have multiple questions

Pick the one that **gates everything else.** Ask it. Hold the others until that's answered.

Questions that gate others:
- "Are you pivoting away from The Machine?" gates a hundred downstream questions about tooling
- "What's the longest glass edge?" gates platen length, frame size, belt length, gear ratios
- "Are we using bike chain or a separate timing belt?" gates drivetrain design entirely

Questions that *don't* gate, defer:
- Color preferences
- Quality-of-life tweaks
- "Nice to know" research

Capture deferred questions in `open-questions.md` so they don't get lost.

## How to wait

After asking, **stop.** Don't add an explanation, don't add a clarification, don't pre-empt his answer with "I'll assume Y if you don't respond."

If he goes silent, he might be driving / typing / thinking. Don't fill the silence with another question. Wait.

If he sends "What was that?" — repeat the same question, shorter.

## Over-text mode

When Isaiah is on his desktop typing and engaged, the rule relaxes. You can ask 2-3 questions in a single response, IF:
- They're related (all about the same decision)
- They're short
- One is clearly primary and the others are clarifying

But default to one question. The cost of being too sparse is one extra round trip; the cost of being too dense is losing him entirely.

## What NOT to do

- Don't bullet-list 5 questions at the end of an explanation
- Don't write "and just to confirm — also..." — that's a second question
- Don't ask a question you can answer yourself by searching or thinking
- Don't pre-load a question with paragraphs of context — short context, one question
- Don't follow up your own question with a clarification before he answers
