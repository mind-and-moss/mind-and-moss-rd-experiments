## Research Summary So Far

### The Machine (flagship concept 1)
- Room-scale zebrafish racetrack aquarium (~55-75 foot continuous loop)
- 10 DIY aquariums (16x8x4-5") stacked in a corner, S-pattern zigzag, gravity-fed cascade
- Structural system: flat acrylic segments + PVC angle connectors + metal bolts (load) + 3D-printed brackets (alignment only) + external cable band
- Key failure modes documented: thermal expansion mismatch, stress cracking at bolts, bracket creep

### The Gem (flagship concept 2)
- Hardscape component: 3D print armature → polymer clay detail → shellac seal → silicone mold → cast final piece (never enters tank)
- Tin-cure silicone required with Sculpey (sulfur content inhibits platinum-cure)
- Glass component: stained glass fused/slumped into hemisphere/sphere form
  - Kiln: Evenheat Studio Pro STP or Paragon SC-2 (~$700-1200 for 6-8" spheres)
  - COE matching critical: Bullseye COE 90 OR System 96 — do not mix
  - Slump molds: Bullseye/Slumpy's/Creative Paradise, 6-12", ~$40-150
- Seam options ranked: 1) UV optical adhesive (invisible), 2) hybrid solder inside/optical adhesive outside, 3) side-glow fiber
- Base material idea: mycelium-bound hempcrete or cork composite (living + structural simultaneously)
- UV adhesive: Norland optical grade for clarity; silicone is inherently UV-stable

### Cylindrical Terrarium (existing setup)
- Sealed cylindrical acrylic tank with bioactive ecosystem (springtails)
- Inside-mount foggy film plan with aquarium silicone corner seal
- Has 24/7 fans and 2-week cure time available — eliminates main failure risks
